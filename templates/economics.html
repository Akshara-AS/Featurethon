  2. Capital in the Twenty-First Century

    Author: Thomas Piketty
    Summary: The book's central thesis is that when the rate of return on capital is greater than the rate of economic growth over the long term, the result is concentration of wealth, and this unequal distribution of wealth causes social and economic instability. Piketty proposes a global system of progressive wealth taxes to help reduce inequality and avoid the vast majority of wealth coming under the control of a tiny minority.
    Genre: Economics
    Year: 30 August 2013
    Language: English
    Also available in French
    Price: 1056 INR (Amazon)
    Rating: 9/10
    Publisher: Harvard Univeersity Press
