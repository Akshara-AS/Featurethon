  1. Arthashastra

    Author: Chanakya
    Summary: The Arthaśāstra is an ancient Indian Sanskrit treatise on statecraft, economic policy and military strategy. Kautilya, also identified as Vishnugupta and Chanakya, is traditionally credited as the author of the text. The latter was a scholar at Takshashila, the teacher and guardian of Emperor Chandragupta Maurya.
    Genre: Statecraft,Treatise, History, Economic policy and Military strategy
    Year: 3rd century BCE
    Language: English
    Originally written in Sanskrit
    Price: 455 INR (Amazon)
    Rating: 9.8/10
    Publisher: Penguin India

  2. Capital in the Twenty-First Century

    Author: Thomas Piketty
    Summary: The book's central thesis is that when the rate of return on capital is greater than the rate of economic growth over the long term, the result is concentration of wealth, and this unequal distribution of wealth causes social and economic instability. Piketty proposes a global system of progressive wealth taxes to help reduce inequality and avoid the vast majority of wealth coming under the control of a tiny minority.
    Genre: Economics
    Year: 30 August 2013
    Language: English
    Also available in French
    Price: 1056 INR (Amazon)
    Rating: 9/10
    Publisher: Harvard Univeersity Press
